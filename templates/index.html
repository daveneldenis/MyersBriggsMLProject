What is Myers-Briggs Personality Indicators?


Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) is one of the most prevalent personality tests in the world. Its purpose is to categorize psychological profiles based on the assumption that variations in everyday behavior are ordered, consistent, and capable of being categorized.

Keep in mind that while MBTI resembles some psychological theories, it is classified as a pseudoscience and is not suppose to be predictive. As the DeepMBTI team, we wanted to bring together something fun and social to you through AI analytics.

Extraversion vs Introversion

What is your orientation, or “attitude,” towards interacting with the world? An extrovert’s primary focus is outward, towards people, objects, and action. An introvert is drawn towards self-reflection, thought, and ideas. Note that it differs slightly from the lay usage of the terms.

Sensing vs Intuition

How do you learn about the world around you? Intuition refers to acquiring and assimilating information not by using concrete methods but following patterns and hunches (“going with your gut”). Sensing is gathering tangible facts (data) through the five senses.

Thinking vs Feeling

How do you make decisions? Thinkers try to detach themselves from situations and make judgments based on logical reasoning. Feelers put themselves in others’ shoes, seeking to make decisions “from the inside.”

Judging vs Perceiving

What is your orientation towards the unknown? Judgment oriented people prefer their world to be structured and their activities to be planned in detail. Perception oriented people are more comfortable with spontaneity, “playing it by ear,” and letting their activities come together naturally.

What is Different about DeepMBTI?


Currently, MBTI is tested with an online survey. The voluntary and conscious nature of the quiz subjects the system to an unrepresentative sample of test takers. Subjects are likely to have a strong interest in the psychology of personality, as well as answer questions with the testing system in mind. Statisticians refer to this confounding variable as voluntary response bias. MBTI’s susceptibility to bias decreases the test’s predictive validity.

Our method eliminates this bias by putting the machine, not the subject, in the driver’s seat. The user puts a passage they wrote prior to using the model into a text interpreter, obviating the risk of this bias.
